Introduction to IoT and Robotics

What is IoT?

IoT stands for Internet of Things. It refers to the network of physical objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet.

What is Robotics?

Robotics is a branch of engineering and science that involves the design, construction, operation, and use of robots. Robots are programmable machines that can perform tasks autonomously or with human guidance.

Applications of IoT and Robotics

IoT Applications

  • Smart Homes: IoT devices can control lighting, heating, and security systems in homes.
  • Healthcare: IoT devices can monitor patients' health and provide real-time data to healthcare providers.
  • Industrial Automation: IoT can optimize manufacturing processes and improve efficiency in factories.

Robotics Applications

  • Manufacturing: Robots are used for assembly lines, welding, and painting in factories.
  • Healthcare: Robots can assist in surgeries, rehabilitation, and patient care.
  • Exploration: Robots are used in space exploration, underwater exploration, and hazardous environments where human presence is risky.
